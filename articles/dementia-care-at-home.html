Dementia Care at Home

How memory games and routines can help slow progression and improve quality of life

Understanding Dementia

Dementia is not a single disease but a general term for the impaired ability to remember, think, or make decisions that interfere with everyday activities. Alzheimer’s disease is the most common cause of dementia. While there is currently no cure, certain activities and routines can help slow progression and improve quality of life.

How In-Home Care Helps

In-home care allows seniors to remain in familiar surroundings, reducing confusion and agitation. Personalized support from caregivers can ensure safety, comfort, and emotional well-being without the need to move into a facility.

Memory-Boosting Activities

  • Puzzles and brain games: Crosswords, Sudoku, and simple logic games stimulate the brain.
  • Reminiscence therapy: Looking at photo albums or talking about past experiences strengthens long-term memory.
  • Music therapy: Playing familiar songs can evoke strong emotional and cognitive responses.
  • Routine and repetition: Establishing daily routines provides a sense of security and reinforces memory.
